One of the questions we hear most often at Supabase is:

"How can I get involved?"

It's a surprisingly difficult question to answer. Not because there aren't opportunities, but because there isn't just one path into the community. Some people contribute code. Others answer questions on Discord. Some write tutorials, organize meetups, test new features, or build tools that thousands of developers use.

If there's one thing I hope you take away from this post, it's this:

If you're excited about Supabase, there's probably a place for you here.

## How community works here

Community at Supabase isn't one group of people — it's an ecosystem. Think of it less as a single community and more as a collection of overlapping groups, each contributing to Supabase in different ways.

```
                Supabase Community

      Build          Help       Teach      Improve     Events
         │             │          │           │          │
         ▼             ▼          ▼           ▼.         ▼
    Contributors    Helpers   Creators   Insiders       Host

           ↔ People move between them ↔
```

People move between these areas, and lots of folks occupy several roles at the same time. We don't expect everyone to contribute in the same way or the same amount. We try to create a place for everyone who wants to make Supabase better.

## Find your place

### Build

Some people contribute by building.

Maybe it's an open source library. Maybe it's an integration with another platform. Maybe it's a starter kit, an MCP server, or a template that helps other developers get started faster.

The best projects never start with "I want to contribute." They start with "I wish this existed." People find the intersection between something they know well—Next.js, Kubernetes, Flutter, Rust, AI agents, etc, and Supabase. They build something they wish existed, and create something useful for other devs.

Every project extends the Supabase ecosystem and makes it more valuable for the next person who discovers it.

If you enjoy building, publishing, and sharing your expertise, there's a place for you here.

### Help

Not every contribution has to be code.

Every day, our helpers answer questions on Discord, Reddit, GitHub Discussions, Stack Overflow, and social media. Sometimes it's a quick pointer to the docs. Sometimes it's spending a hours helping someone debug a tricky issue with their project.

If you enjoy helping people solve problems, there's a place for you here.

### Teach

One of the most impactful ways to contribute is by teaching. Maybe it's a post on Reddit or on your own blog explaining a feature you just learned. Maybe it's a video for YouTube or TikTok, a conference talk, a workshop, or a course. Maybe it's simply documenting the solution to a problem you spent hours figuring out yourself.

The best educational content comes from devs who remember what it felt like to be stuck. If something confused you, chances are it will confuse someone else too.

Every tutorial, guide, and video makes Supabase easier to learn and more accessible to the next dev.

If you enjoy teaching and sharing what you learn, there's a place for you here.

### Improve

Some of the best feedback we get comes from people who simply use it every day.

They notice the rough edges, report bugs, suggest new features, participate in betas, and tell us how it could all be better. Sometimes it's a detailed bug report, a friction log, or a GitHub discussion. Sometimes it's just a simple: "This part was confusing."

We've shipped countless improvements to the the Supabase DX as a result of conversations with community users.

If you use the product, are very opinionated, and enjoy making products better, there's a place for you here.

## There's No Single Path

When people ask how to get involved, they're often looking for a roadmap. _Do X, then Y, then Z._ Or _Do X for 3 months_ or someting like that. Community doesn't really work that way. Some people become known for answering questions on Discord. Others build a library that thousands of developers use. Some write tutorials. Some organize meetups. Some become the go-to expert for a particular framework or technology.

Everyone's path is different because everyone brings something different to the community.

The goal for you isn't to climb a ladder. It's to find the intersection between what you're enjoy, what you're good at, and where you can have the biggest impact. The goal for us is to help you get there.

Over time, opportunities naturally follow. You might get invited into the SupaSquad, join an Insiders program, collaborate with the team on a project, speak at or host an event, or even work at Supabase.

Those opportunities aren't the destination—they're recognition of the value you've already created.

## Recognizing great contributors

Community isn't built on goodwill alone.

We believe that people who make Supabase better should be recognized for the value they create. Sometimes that recognition is simply amplifying a great project or introducing someone to the team. Other times, it's paying people to help them move forward.

There's no one-size-fits-all approach. Different kinds of contributions need different kinds of support.

#### Builders & Open Source Contributors

We love open source, and we know that maintaining it takes real time and effort.

We fund community projects, sponsor maintainers, and pay developers for work that benefits the wider Supabase ecosystem through Open Collective. Sometimes that's building an integration, maintaining a library, or contributing to open source software that our community depends on.

Our goal isn't just to celebrate great work—we want to help sustain it.

#### Helpers

Helping other developers is valuable work.

Our best helpers are often experts in Postgres, frontend frameworks, Flutter, AI, Kubernetes, or other technologies in the Supabase ecosystem. They bring deep technical knowledge, but more importantly, they're generous with it.

Community members who consistently answer questions, mentor others, and improve the experience for everyone are often invited into programs like the SupaSquad, where we can fund and recognize their impact.

#### Content Creators

Great educational content takes time.

Whether it's a tutorial, a YouTube video, or a creative project, we regularly commission community creators to produce content that helps developers learn Supabase in new ways.

We'd much rather work with creators who genuinely love the product than make everything ourselves.

#### Event Hosts

Some of the best communities are built face-to-face.

If you're passionate about bringing developers together, we'll help you make it happen. We provide organizers with budget, resources, and the freedom to run meetups, workshops, hackathons, and other events that make sense for their local communities.

The best events aren't copies of ours—they're shaped by the people who know their communities best.

## Opportunities Follow Contributions

When people create value for the ecosystem, we look for opportunities to give something back. Sometimes that's recognition. Sometimes it's early access, closer collaboration with the team, or an invitation to join the SupaSquad. Often, it's money.

We fund open source maintainers through Open Collective, commission tutorials and videos, pay developers to build integrations and tools, and provide budgets for meetup organizers to run the events they want to create.

Over time, those contributions also build something that's hard to fake: a public track record. We've seen contributors become contractors, long-term collaborators, and occasionally full-time employees. Tomas, one of the authors of this post, started by helping people on Discord before eventually joining Supabase full-time.

## Where Do I Start?

The best way to get involved is to pick something that genuinely interests you.

Build a library. Answer a question on Discord. Write a tutorial. Organize a meetup. Join a beta. Tell us where the product could be better.

Start small. The goal is to contribute consistently in a way that's enjoyable and sustainable.

After you've been contributing for a while, you might decide to [apply to the SupaSquad](https://supabase.com/supasquad). It's our way of recognizing people who have already made a meaningful impact on the community and want to become more involved.
